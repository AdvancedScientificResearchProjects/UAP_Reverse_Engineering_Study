# Bob Lazar Public Appearances 2006–2018: The Quiet Era

Research compiled: 2026-04-13

This covers all known interviews, documentary segments, podcast appearances, conference talks, and public statements by Bob Lazar from 2006 through late 2018 (when the Corbell documentary re-ignited mainstream interest). The period is characterized by very few appearances — Lazar was deliberately keeping a low profile and running United Nuclear Scientific Equipment in Michigan.

- Any public response from Lazar specifically about the 2003 synthesis of Element 115 (Moscovium) — he was largely silent on this confirmation in public record

**Likely gap:** The period roughly 2007–2013 is the deepest "quiet" stretch. The federal plea in 2007 (United Nuclear fined $7,500, three years probation) appears to have contributed to Lazar's withdrawal from public life. The next confirmed direct Lazar interview after the 2009 C2C anniversary show is the May 2014 KLAS segment. That is approximately 4–5 years of near-total silence.

## Element 115 / Moscovium Timeline Note

Lazar claimed since 1989 that the alien craft used a stable, super-heavy element with 115 protons as its power source — an element that did not appear on the periodic table at the time. Scientists in Russia and the US first synthesized atoms of element 115 in February 2004 (published in *Physical Review C*). It was formally named Moscovium and added to the periodic table in 2016. Lazar did not appear to make any notable public statement in response to the 2004 synthesis or 2016 naming. The critical scientific distinction — that produced isotopes of Moscovium decay in ~100 milliseconds and are profoundly unstable, while Lazar described a stable isotope — has been used by skeptics to distinguish the prediction from the confirmation.
