# Bob Lazar Appearances 1989–2005: S-4/Area 51 Claims

Chronological list of known interviews, TV segments, radio shows, documentary appearances,
conference presentations, and print interviews related to Bob Lazar's claims about S-4,
antigravity propulsion, Element 115, the Sport Model craft, and reactor technology.

Sources used: Papoose Lake Institute archive, Otherhand.org Lazar timeline, Coast to Coast AM
guest page, KLAS/Mystery Wire archive, Internet Archive, IMDb, Norio Hayakawa blog, UFOMind,
and targeted web searches.

---

## 1989

### 1. KLAS-TV — "On the Record" silhouette interview ("Dennis")
- **Date:** May 15, 1989
- **Show/Outlet:** KLAS-TV (Las Vegas), 5 p.m. newscast, segment of George Knapp's "On the Record" slot
- **Interviewer:** George Knapp
- **Format:** Live TV, video
- **Duration:** ~5 minutes (nightly news segment)
- **Topics:** Nine craft in hangars at S-4 south of Groom Lake; Navy-controlled program; reverse engineering; nine flying saucers of alien origin
- **Notes:** Lazar's face hidden in silhouette; pseudonym "Dennis" used (his supervisor's name at S-4); segment prompted international radio rebroadcast in six European countries
- **Archive link:** https://archive.org/details/bob-lazar-interview-1989 (compilation including this segment)

### 2. KLAS-TV — "UFOs: The Best Evidence" nine-part series (identity revealed)
- **Date:** November 6–25, 1989 (series ran across multiple broadcasts; Lazar named and shown on air November 10, 1989 in Part 5)
- **Show/Outlet:** KLAS-TV (Las Vegas)
- **Interviewer:** George Knapp
- **Format:** TV news documentary series, video
- **Duration:** Nine parts; Part 5 (November 10) included lie-detector test segment; November 25 two-hour compilation special
- **Topics:** Lazar's identity revealed; lie-detector test; S-4 facility description; nine craft; Sport Model dimensions (~30–35 ft diameter, ~15 ft tall); Element 115 as fuel; gravity amplifiers; Navy oversight; EG&G job interview; security clearance 38 levels above Q clearance
- **Notes:** Became highest-rated local news broadcast in Las Vegas history. November 25 broadcast was a two-hour compilation of the series. Within days, covered internationally including a Japanese TV special.
- **Archive links:**
  - https://www.mysterywire.com/ufo/bob-lazar-describes-alien-technology-housed-at-secret-s-4-base-in-nevada-part-5/ (Part 5 on Mystery Wire)
  - https://www.mysterywire.com/ufo/ufos-the-best-evidence/ (overview)
  - https://archive.org/details/bob-lazar-interview-1989 (archived video)

### 3. Billy Goodman Happening — KVEG 840 AM (first appearance)
- **Date:** November 21–22, 1989 (10:00 p.m. to 1:00 a.m., overnight)
- **Show/Outlet:** "The Billy Goodman Happening," KVEG 840 AM, Las Vegas
- **Interviewer:** Billy Goodman (host); open phone-in format
- **Format:** Live radio, audio
- **Duration:** ~3 hours
- **Topics:** Area S-4 vs. Area 51 distinction (S-4 is 10 miles south of Groom Lake); Sport Model craft dimensions; gravity amplifiers and space-time bending; three generators on bottom of craft; nine saucers total; Lazar touched craft exterior metal; stood inside doorway; Wednesday night test flights; 38 levels above Q clearance; quantum physics questions from callers; underground bases at Mercury; alien appearance questions
- **Notes:** Bob Lazar and Gene Huff were in-studio guests together. First major radio exposure.
- **Archive links:**
  - https://www.papooselake.org/interview-transcripts/bob-lazar-on-the-billy-goodman-happening-2 (transcript)
  - https://gorightly.wordpress.com/2009/01/19/the-billy-goodman-happening/ (context)

### 4. KLAS-TV — "On the Record" follow-up interview
- **Date:** December 9, 1989
- **Show/Outlet:** KLAS-TV, "On The Record" hosted by George Knapp
- **Interviewer:** George Knapp
- **Format:** TV studio interview, video
- **Duration:** ~30 minutes (extended segment)
- **Topics:** Background at Los Alamos National Lab; hiring process through EG&G; S-4 location (10–15 miles south of Groom Lake, ~125 miles north of Las Vegas); job as senior staff physicist; Navy as controlling agency; one month follow-up to series launch
- **Notes:** This is the extended sit-down interview often circulated online. Lazar is now fully identified and on camera.
- **Archive links:**
  - https://www.papooselake.org/filmed-interviews (listed as "On The Record with George Knapp")
  - https://archive.org/details/bob-lazar-interview-1989

### 5. Billy Goodman Happening — KVEG 840 AM (second appearance)
- **Date:** December 20, 1989
- **Show/Outlet:** "The Billy Goodman Happening," KVEG 840 AM, Las Vegas
- **Interviewer:** Billy Goodman
- **Format:** Live radio, audio
- **Duration:** Not precisely documented; estimated ~2–3 hours (standard show format)
- **Topics:** Follow-up to November appearance; caller questions; continued discussion of S-4, propulsion, craft details

## 1990

### 6. Nippon Television Network (NTV) Japan — Area 51 special (filmed interview)
- **Date filmed:** February 21, 1990 (at Lazar's Las Vegas residence; arranged by Norio Hayakawa)
- **Date aired:** Mid-March 1990 (two-hour prime-time TV special in Japan)
- **Show/Outlet:** Nippon Television Network (NTV), Japan
- **Interviewer:** NTV crew (Norio Hayakawa facilitated)
- **Format:** TV documentary segment, video
- **Duration:** Interview session was ~4 hours; broadcast segment unknown; overall special was ~2 hours
- **Topics:** S-4 facility; craft descriptions; Element 115; propulsion system; Lazar gave crew directions to Highway 375 (the Black Mailbox area) to observe test flights that same night
- **Notes:** Lazar did not travel to Japan to appear live as originally planned, claiming he was "not allowed." The interview footage was incorporated into the Japanese broadcast. This was the first major international TV coverage of Lazar's claims.
- **Source:** Norio Hayakawa's account at https://noriohayakawa.wordpress.com/2022/11/20/my-involvement-with-area-51-research-norio-hayakawa/

### 7. John Andrews interview (Testors model designer)
- **Date:** November 25, 1990
- **Show/Outlet:** Private interview; John Andrews was Testors model designer investigating Lazar's craft descriptions
- **Format:** Filmed/audio interview (private)
- **Duration:** Unknown
- **Topics:** Sport Model craft dimensions, external appearance, interior layout; served as basis for the 1994 Testors S-4 Saucer model kit
- **Archive links:**
  - https://www.papooselake.org/filmed-interviews (listed as "1990 John Andrews Interview (11-25-1990)")
  - https://www.slideshare.net/slideshow/john-andrews-investigation-correspondence-of-bob-lazarpdf/252201452 (Andrews' correspondence PDF)

### 8. Text interviews published in books (1990)
- **Dates:** Various, 1990
- **Outlet:** Three separate book interviews/chapters
  - *Revelations* by Jacques Vallée (Ballantine Books, 1991) — interview conducted 1990
  - *Alien Contact* by Timothy Good (William Morrow, 1991) — interview conducted March/April 1990
  - *UFOs and the Alien Presence* by Michael Lindemann — interview dated September 22, 1990
- **Format:** Text/print (published in books)
- **Topics:** Credentials, S-4 technical claims, propulsion physics, Element 115, craft interior, security protocols
- **Archive links:**
  - https://www.papooselake.org/interview-transcripts (transcripts linked via Scribd)
  - https://docs.preterhuman.net/Interview_with_Bob_Lazar_on_Area_51 (text version)

## 1991

### 9. "The Lazar Tape and Excerpts from the Government Bible" (VHS)
- **Date:** 1991 (released as VHS; exact production date within 1991 not confirmed)
- **Show/Outlet:** Self-produced by Gene Huff; distributed independently on VHS
- **Interviewer/Director:** Gene Huff
- **Format:** Video (originally VHS); now digitized
- **Duration:** ~40 minutes
- **Topics:** Full account of S-4; Project Galileo (gravity propulsion); Project Sidekick (beam weapon with neutron source focused by gravity lens); Project Looking Glass (physics of seeing back in time); Element 115 / Ununpentium; reactor design; Sport Model interior (no right angles, no seams, no buttons, all one color, three seats, central reactor); antigravity wave generation
- **Notes:** The most technically detailed on-camera account Lazar gave during this era. Often cited as the primary primary-source document for his technical claims. Available on Amazon Prime.
- **Archive links:**
  - https://archive.org/details/antigravity (Internet Archive)
  - https://archive.org/details/bob-lazar-on-area-51-and-antigravity-1991
  - https://www.imdb.com/title/tt8027166/ (IMDb listing)

## 1993

### 11. "The Ultimate UFO Seminar" at Little A-Le-Inn, Rachel, Nevada (Q&A)
- **Date:** May 1, 1993
- **Show/Outlet:** Conference organized by Norio Hayakawa and Gary Schultz; "The Ultimate UFO Seminar"
- **Format:** Live public Q&A (filmed by attendees; hidden camera recording exists)
- **Duration:** ~1–2 hours
- **Topics:** S-4, craft descriptions, propulsion, Element 115, government secrecy; Lazar answered nearly all questions; no prepared formal presentation
- **Notes:** Lazar arrived in a car with Nevada plate "MJ-12." Described as his first true public speaking appearance before an audience (~250 people). He was cordial but visibly uncomfortable in the spotlight. A full transcript was published and sold by Glenn Campbell for $8 + postage. A MUFON Journal article also covered the event.
- **Archive links:**
  - https://www.bitchute.com/video/oglp5nlYc8d5/ (hidden camera footage)

### 15. "UFO Line" radio show — KLAV 1230 AM, Las Vegas (recurring)
- **Dates:** December 8, 1995 through January 12, 1996 (Fridays, 11:00 p.m. to midnight; six consecutive weeks)
- **Show/Outlet:** "UFO Line" — Lazar and Huff purchased the slot themselves on KLAV 1230 AM, Las Vegas
- **Format:** Live radio, call-in, audio
- **Duration:** ~1 hour per episode (six episodes total)
- **Topics:** Open-line UFO discussion; caller questions about S-4, craft, propulsion; Lazar and Huff characterized by casual, dismissive attitude toward more outlandish caller claims; John Lear appeared as a guest on at least one episode
- **Notes:** Lazar and Gene Huff independently bought the airtime — no commercial plugs, no 900 number, no merchandise. Described in UFOMind's Desert Rat newsletter as "refreshingly different." Known episode dates: December 8, December 15, December 22, 1995; January episodes through January 12, 1996. A recording of the December 1995 episode has re-emerged.

## Notes on Gaps and Uncertain Appearances

### Possible ~1995 Paul McKenna appearance
The Papoose Lake Institute lists a ~1995 Paul McKenna appearance separately from the confirmed 1997 one. It may refer to a different McKenna series (e.g., "The Hypnotic World of Paul McKenna," 1993–97) or may be a misdating of the 1997 episode. Not independently confirmed.

### Larry King Live (CNN)
Multiple sources refer to a Larry King appearance, but the confirmed appearance is the **2019** "Larry King Now" (internet show, with Jeremy Corbell) — not the original CNN "Larry King Live." No confirmed CNN Larry King Live appearance in the 1989–2005 window has been found. The claim of a mid-1990s Larry King Live appearance circulates in UFO forums but lacks a specific date or episode record.

### 1992 "Need to Know Seminar" at Little A-Le-Inn
Norio Hayakawa organized this event; Bob Lazar was in the audience. Not a speaking appearance — he attended as a spectator. Not counted as a formal media event.

### 1990 Text interviews published in books
Three extended interviews published in 1990–1991 books (Vallée's *Revelations*, Timothy Good's *Alien Contact*, Michael Lindemann's *UFOs and the Alien Presence*) are text only, not audio or video, but represent significant documented primary source material. See entries above.

### 2004–2005 period
No additional confirmed appearances between the October 2004 "Conspiracy?" episode and the April 2006 Coast to Coast AM appearance have been found. Lazar appears to have been largely withdrawn from media in 2004–2005 while running United Nuclear.
