## D. Propulsion — Gravity A / Gravity B, Modes, Amplifiers

- Two distinct gravities. Gravity A = atomic/micro scale (holds protons and neutrons together; currently labeled "strong nuclear force"). Gravity B = planetary/macro scale (what we already know).
  - KLAS-89b [00:20:39].
  - LT-91 [00:10:11], [00:10:44].
- Graviton theory dismissed: "total nonsense."
  - LT-91 [00:09:57].
- In heavy-enough stable nuclei, the cumulative Gravity A wave extends past the atomic perimeter where it can be accessed and amplified.
  - LT-91 [00:14:00]–[00:14:47].
  - BG-89 [00:28:38].
- Amplified Gravity A wave compared to black-hole-level intensity.
  - LT-91 [00:15:16].
- Craft generates its own gravitational field. Occupants are "in a distortion" so inertia doesn't affect them during sharp maneuvers.
  - KLAS-89b [00:14:26], [00:15:02].
- Three gravity amplifiers. Near-Earth operation: one amplifier lifts; the other two swing forward to create a downhill distortion the craft rolls into (low-speed hovering/travel).
  - KLAS-89b [00:15:25]–[00:16:48].
- Delta = all three amplifiers focused on a single point (space travel). Omicron = one (near-planet lift).
  - KLAS-89b [00:15:40].
  - LT-91 [00:29:30]: "…in the delta configuration, and when only one is being used for travel, it's in the omicron configuration."
- Amplifiers are **pulsed**, not continuous, and can be focused independently.
  - LT-91 [00:29:23].
- In Delta-space-travel mode, craft tilts up on its side; focuses the three emitters on the target point; distortion folds over into a heart-shape around the top (360° = donut from above). At maximum distortion the craft is invisible from any vantage point.
  - KLAS-89b [00:15:51]–[00:16:06].
  - LT-91 [00:29:50]–[00:30:35].
- Light bending around the craft: from below you can see what is above it (gravity lensing).
  - KLAS-89b [00:21:20].
- Low-speed mode unstable because Earth's gravity field varies with subsurface mineralogy/density.
  - KLAS-89b [00:17:13].
- BG-89 description of the two modes: low-speed craft "sitting on three gravity waves" is vulnerable / bobs around; high-speed mode: amplitude raised → roll maneuver → bottom aimed at destination → three amplifiers converge on target point → full power → time/space distortion "whips them right to that point."
  - BG-89 [00:44:51]–[00:45:36].

## L. Internal contradictions summary (within 1989–1991 corpus)

1. **Sport-model diameter.** 52 ft (KLAS-89b, 1989-11-10) vs 40 ft (LT-91, 1991) vs "mid-30 to 40 feet" (BG-89, 1989-12-20). 1991 account also adds 16 ft height, not given earlier.
2. **Schooling.** KLAS narration attributes MIT + Caltech (1989-11-10); Lazar on-air declines to name the school (BG-89, 1989-12-20); 1991 only states "degrees in physics and electronics technology" plus Los Alamos Meson Physics Facility.
3. **Sidekick weapon type.** 1989: "particle beam" with gravity-lens focusing (KLAS-89b). 1991: "beam weapon that had a **neutron source**… focused by a gravity lens" (LT-91).
4. **Seeing an alien.** Firm "no" on-camera (KLAS-89b [00:07:24]) vs acknowledged "fleeting glimpse once" on BG-89 [00:23:53] — Lazar refuses to make the claim but will not fully disavow it.
5. **Briefings count.** 121 or 122 (KLAS-89b) vs "120 or so" (LT-91) — effectively consistent, noted for completeness.
6. **S-4 reactor vs the "S-4" near Tonopah test range.** In BG-89 [00:00:06] Lazar clarifies multiple "S-4"s exist on the Nevada Test Site (one is "a nuclear reactor"). This is not a contradiction per se but clarifies potential confusion with other mentions.
7. **Origin system specificity.** KLAS-89b [00:08:01] says craft from Zeta Reticuli binary (no planet specified). LT-91 [00:33:07] escalates to **Reticulum 4 / Zeta Reticuli 2 / ~30 light years** with day-length figure. The 1991 account contains additional briefing-document specificity not mentioned in 1989.
8. **Gravity-wave resonant frequency.** BG-89 [00:22:55]: Lazar says he knows it but can't recall, describes it as "a really odd frequency." No numerical value ever given in the 1989–1991 corpus.
9. **Atomic weight of 115.** BG-89 [01:33:44]: Lazar says he has written it down and has calculated it, but will not give a ballpark on air. No numerical value ever given in the 1989–1991 corpus.
