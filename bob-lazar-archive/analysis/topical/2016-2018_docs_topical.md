# Lazar 2016 & 2018 Corbell Docs — Topical Consolidation

Claims from the two Corbell films, grouped by topic. Each item flagged **CONSISTENT** (matches pre-2016 canon), **EXPANDED** (new detail on an old claim), or **NEW** (first-time claim on camera). The 2018 feature is Lazar's most polished on-camera account between 1991 and 2019, so details often differ from radio-format improv; flagged items are potential anchors/contradictions for the MASTER.

Sources:
- **CW16** = Cosmic Whistleblower, 2016-02-19 (File 38)
- **BL18** = Bob Lazar: Area 51 & Flying Saucers, 2018-12-04 (File 40)

## 1. Element 115

### 1.1 Fuel of reactor, triangular form — CONSISTENT
- BL18 [01:25:25] *"Element 115 was what we would call the fuel."*
- BL18 [01:26:14] *"This fuel is in the form of a just a three-dimensional thin triangle, little rounded edges. It's somewhat copper in color, you know, that reddish-brownish."* — copper color matches JRE2479.

### 1.2 Produces its own gravitational energy — CONSISTENT
- BL18 [01:25:32] *"Element 115 affects gravity. Element 115 produces its own gravitational energy."*

### 1.3 Machining process — EXPANDED
- BL18 [01:26:14–27:50] Fullest on-camera recipe: cylinder → machined to cone → sliced at angles into triangles. AND: the starting cylinder is itself *"many disks stacked up… fused together."* Matches Rachel-93 but with added "stacked-disk fused cylinder" precursor step. *"Now, apparently, if you don't do all this, that doesn't work."*

### 1.4 Offsite code-name "LA-1000" — NEW
- BL18 [01:25:46] *"Its code name was LA-1000. That's what it was referred to offsite."*
- BL18 [01:25:53] Cover purpose: *"supposed to be an advanced armor."*
- **Not found in 1989–2009 or 2019+ corpus in these reviewed transcripts. Specific to BL18.**

### 1.5 Energy density — NEW phrasing (CW16)
- CW16 [00:12:22] *"a kilogram of antimatter is equal in energy to 47 megaton hydrogen bombs."* — specific "47 megaton" figure is new phrasing; prior corpus uses other comparators.

### 1.6 FBI raid was seeking 115 — CONSISTENT with LK19
- BL18 [01:18:00–21:04]: Knapp asserts on camera; Lazar pointedly declines to answer whether he removed a sample, rather than denying.

### 1.7 Stability can't be ruled out — Knapp vindication
- BL18 [01:23:25] Knapp canvassed top heavy-element physicists worldwide: *"All of them across the board said, you cannot rule out a stabilized version of element 115."* Preserved here as on-camera expert-consult record, supporting Lazar's stable-isotope claim.
