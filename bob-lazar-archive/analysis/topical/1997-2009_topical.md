## 1. Element 115

### 1.1 Identity, stability, form

- **L 97 [02:15:21]:** "A super heavy element… doesn't naturally occur on Earth… we haven't synthesized." Stable, no radioactive decay. References theoretical "island of stability" around 114/115/116.
- **L 03 [01:31:00]:** "Something very unique about the fuel… incredibly heavy and incredibly dense."
- **H 09 [01:25:49]:** "It wasn't just 115. I believe it was an **isotope** of 115. And… it had to be kept under **neutron bombardment at rest**."
- **H 09 [01:27:41]:** "The 115 Bob worked with wasn't just 115 like hydrogen is hydrogen."

**Evolution from 1989:** In 1989 Lazar described element 115 as stable. By 2009 (via Huff), the claim is refined to a specific isotope requiring neutron bombardment to remain in its useful state. This post-hoc refinement protects the original story against the 2004 Dubna synthesis, which produced only extremely short-lived 115 isotopes.

### 1.2 Quantity

- **L 97 [02:17:20]:** "223 grams in the craft, in the reactor."
- **Knapp/H 09 [01:25:20]:** Ballpark "500 pounds of this stuff"; Huff qualifies "500 pounds isn't a roomful. This is an element heavier than lead."

**Internal contradiction:** 223 g in the reactor (1997) vs. 500-pound programme stockpile (2009, Knapp phrasing) — these may be reconcilable (reactor charge vs. entire stockpile) but Lazar never clarified on-air.

### 1.3 Reaction sequence (fuel → gravity wave + heat)

- **J 98 [01:26:47]:** "Pumping protons into 115, it becomes 116, which immediately decays… the result of the decay is antimatter… 100% conversion of matter to energy… also out of this reaction, they pull off the gravity A wave." (Lear's formulation.)
- **L 03 [01:32:25]:** "The reactor worked in the craft was like a small accelerator… constantly bombarded the 115 which transmuted and immediately decayed and that's when it produced its gravitational pulses and as a by-product it produced a tremendous amount of heat."
- **L 03 [01:33:27]:** "Heat is converted to electricity" — drives the craft's electrical subsystems. Electrical power is a by-product; gravitational pulses are the primary output.
- **H 09 [01:26:10]:** Proton sent "down a tuned tube… to plug into the nucleus of the 115, which would bend up to 116… fission off some particles, some of which were antimatter. And this antimatter would be reacted with matter. And then the heat from that reaction would be converted to electricity."
- **H 09 [01:54:45]:** The matter target is a **gas** inside the reactor (unspecified composition: "could be hydrogen, could be anything").

**Consistency:** Lazar's 2003 description matches the Lear/Huff 115→116→antimatter framing, though Lazar himself does not explicitly use the word "antimatter" in the 2003 transcript when describing the reactor (he says "transmuted and immediately decayed").

### 1.4 Synthesis on Earth

- **L 97 [02:17:13]:** "The last isolated was 112… most of the more recent work has been done in Darmstadt, Germany." Darmstadt plan: fuse curium + calcium to make 116.
- **L 03 [01:31:27]:** Darmstadt "were sneaking up on 115… using a new technique instead of bombarding something with neutrons, they were just slamming nuclei together… I don't think they reached it… somebody found out that there was false data. The last heavy element that was claimed that might have been 113."
- **H 09 [01:27:05]:** "Since then, yes, they have created the 115 in a lab. But… it had to exist for X amount of seconds or fractions of seconds and then it decays."

**Critical note on the task brief:** The brief claims element 115 was "just synthesized in Russia" at the time of the 2003 Dec 06 interview. That is not correct: the Dubna announcement of moscovium (115) synthesis was on February 2, 2004 — about **8 weeks after** this interview. Therefore the 2003 transcript contains **no response from Lazar to Russian synthesis of element 115**, because the Russian synthesis had not yet been announced. Lazar's 2003 commentary about heavy-element synthesis refers to Darmstadt, Germany and to the false-data retractions (the 2002 Berkeley retraction of the Ninov 118 data).

### 1.5 Origin / cosmology

- **J 09 [01:29:37]:** "Naturally occurring element 115 cannot be found anywhere near us. It can only be found in a solar system much larger." Large solar-system formation required (more EM energy and mass).
- **J 09 [02:04:48]:** "It cannot be made" in useful quantities — too costly.

### 1.6 Physical samples in Lazar's hands

- **L 97 [02:17:46]:** Declines to comment on whether he removed some.
- **H 09 [01:21:14]:** "Somebody gave it to him from Los Alamos… we put out the story that he stole it. So we wouldn't implicate the guy that gave it to us."
- **H/J 09 [01:21:04]:** Cloud-chamber experiment at Lazar's house — bell jar, dry ice, 115 sample, thorium Coleman mantle as alpha source. Alpha particles observed to deflect. Videotaped; tape subsequently misplaced.
- **J 09 [01:30:32]:** Three pieces originally. "Two were stolen. One we have no comment on." Bob Bigelow involved in recovery attempt. One recovered sample was buried at an undisclosed secure location where tampering would be visible.

**Contradiction:** 1989/1997 Lazar explicitly declined to say whether he had any; by 2009, Huff/Lear openly describe a sample he held and experimented with. Lazar himself never confirms this on air in 97 or 03.

## 2. Gravity physics

### 2.1 Gravity wave character

- **L 97 [02:50:22]:** "There was an apparent carrier wave… extremely low frequency… 7.46 Hertz at a one micron bandwidth."
- **L 97 [02:51:04]:** "Narrow band phenomena"; "Gravity radiation is coherent."
- **L 97 [01:36:00]:** "Gravity waves behave a lot more like microwaves… waveguides and things of that sort."
- **L 03 [01:25:16]:** "Almost seemed to propagate as microwaves did, since anytime inside the craft, the gravity, the basic gravity wave was routed… always traveled inside tuned pipes."
- **L 03 [01:24:43]:** "I don't believe there are gravitons. I don't believe there's particles involved. I don't believe it's even a wave-particle duality."

### 2.2 Propagation speed

- **L 03 [01:27:50]:** "The propagation is instantaneously. Every way we attempted to measure the propagation from the reactor base to the emitters themselves, there was no delay at all."
- **L 03 [01:28:13]:** Caveat — possibly the wave's own gravity field is distorting time-space in transit and only appearing instantaneous.
- **J 98 [02:24:50]:** "Gravity is instantaneous, and there's two forms of it, A and B." (Lear.)

### 2.3 Gravity A and Gravity B

- **J 98 [01:27:28]:** "They pull off the gravity A wave" (Lear).
- **J 98 [02:25:00]:** Two forms A and B (Lear).
- Lazar himself does **not** use the "gravity A" / "gravity B" labels in either 97 or 03. This terminology is Lear's. (It does appear in Lazar's 1991 "Lazar Tape" but in this era it is consistently Lear who voices it.) The 1997 on-air exchange with Australian caller Stan Dale is explicitly about a toroidal field and Lazar's "gravity wave A and gravity wave B focus fields" concept referenced in Lazar's own video, but Lazar does not define A vs. B on-air in these 4 transcripts.

### 2.4 Field geometry

- **L 97 [01:31:18]:** Toroidal field around the craft. Pedestal extension down below in omicron hover.
- **L 97 [02:55:00]:** "Directly under the craft, you will only see the sky above it" — light is bent around, producing camouflage. "From certain viewpoints, the craft is completely invisible."
- **L 97 [03:01:00]:** Blue-corona discharge at bottom during lift-off, from high voltage; dissipates above ~30 ft altitude.

### 2.5 Gravity-distortion demos

- **L 97 [01:45:58]:** Emitter focused → "produced a little black dot in the air, hovering in the air." Only gravity can bend light to that degree.
- **L 97 [02:57:02]:** Candle-in-distortion experiment. Candle continued to emit visible light but the flame "was flickering as if it was frozen in time." Lazar repeatedly says this "drives me crazy" — he cannot reconcile localized time dilation with continued photon emission.
- **L 97 [01:42:02]:** Hand pushed back from the operating reactor sphere like opposing magnet poles; golf ball ricocheted off the field (omnidirectional anti-gravitational field).

## 3. Propulsion — amplifiers, emitters, waveguides

### 3.1 Components

- **L 97 [01:34:58]:** Reactor + one amplifier on workbench in lab; amplifier "about two feet in diameter, probably four feet long, cylindrical… plates on the outside, black in color."
- **L 97 [01:35:47]:** Corrects terminology: on the workbench was actually the **emitter**, not the amplifier. The emitter is the device that pivots and radiates the gravity wave, analogous to a microwave horn.
- **L 97 [02:00:57]:** In the craft, **three emitters** hang below (2 ft × 4 ft cylinders), connected via short (~12 in) solid metal pipes to **three rectangular amplifier consoles** on the main level. The pipe bends when charged but stays rigid — "a solid piece… applying an electric charge and having the pipe bend, but remain rigid after it had done so." Not telescoping.
- **L 97 [01:57:06]:** Amplifier consoles have **no buttons/lights/switches/wiring**.
- **L 97 [01:56:50]:** Central reactor, with waveguide chimney rising to top of craft skin.

### 3.2 Flight modes

- **L 97 [02:03:41] / L 03 [00:11:49]:** **Omicron** = atmospheric. One emitter points down as "anti-gravitational pillar"; the other two tilt forward to create a gravitational distortion ahead. Craft falls "downhill" toward the distortion.
- **L 97 [02:06:19] / L 03 [00:12:15]:** **Delta** = interstellar. Belly forward. All three emitters focus on a single distant point. Fabric of space bent toward craft; craft "moves without actually traveling."
- **L 03 [00:12:42]:** New framing — UFOs seen at 45° angles are transitioning between omicron and delta modes.

### 3.3 Power levels / timing

- **L 97 [02:52:00]:** "Gigawatts." Reactor always at full output; throttling is by phasing the amplifiers.
- **L 97 [02:53:03]:** Delta-mode jumps come in consecutive cycles; can't bridge, e.g., 30 light-years in a single jump. Recycle time "microseconds or a millisecond."
- **J 09 [01:48:53]:** Lear-version recycle time: **12 milliseconds**. (More specific than Lazar's recollection; source unclear — may be a Lear elaboration.)

### 3.4 Propulsion theory

- **L 97 [02:06:19]:** "Bend, you begin to distort space and time, and instead of the craft actually flying, you're basically distorting the fabric of space around the craft."
- **L 97 [02:07:56]:** Time slows in strong gravitational field — not a time machine, "just a byproduct."
- **L 03 [00:14:02]:** Craft essentially immune to external forces when own field is active — cannot crash in a storm, only from internal failure or pilot error.

### 3.5 Scale of power envelope

- **L 97 [01:40:40]:** Reactor the size of roughly "two foot square, maybe six inches high" (base plate). Huff recalls output compared to Hoover Dam (Lazar doesn't confirm the exact number).

## 10. Moscovium / Russian synthesis reaction

**There is no Lazar-voiced reaction to moscovium synthesis in the 1997–2009 corpus.**

- The Dubna synthesis of 115 was announced February 2, 2004, ~8 weeks after the December 6, 2003 C2C appearance. Lazar's 2003 comments about heavy-element synthesis reference Darmstadt, Germany and the ~2002 Berkeley retraction of the Ninov 118 data.
- In the 2009 show Lazar was absent; Huff (not Lazar) provides the only retrospective commentary, and he explicitly marks the mismatch: "Now there is [115]. However, the 115 that's been created in a lab is far different from what Bob Lazar described back then." [09, 01:24:23]. Huff reconciles by saying Bob's 115 was an isotope requiring neutron bombardment to remain stable.

**Flag:** Any claim that Lazar on-air directly addressed the Russian synthesis is not supported by these four transcripts. The task brief's premise on this point should be revised or traced to a different interview.

## 11. Contradictions and evolution

- **Stability of 115.** 1989/1997 Lazar: flatly stable. 2009 Huff: isotope requiring neutron bombardment at rest. (Post-hoc defense against Dubna results.)
- **Amplifier vs. emitter terminology.** Lazar himself conflates them in 97, then corrects on-air [01:35:47]: the pivoting horn is the emitter; the rectangular console is the amplifier.
- **Recycle time in delta mode.** Lazar 97: "microseconds or a millisecond" (could not recall). Lear 09: 12 ms.
- **Samples in hand.** Lazar 97/03: no first-person confirmation. Huff/Lear 09: three pieces, cloud-chamber experiment, provenance from a Los Alamos insider (cover story of theft), two stolen, one buried.
- **Project names.** Never named by Lazar in 97 or 03 on air. First explicit public naming is by Huff in 09 (Galileo, Sidekick, Looking Glass).
- **Time-travel / Looking Glass.** Lazar never describes Looking Glass in these four transcripts. Huff/Knapp 09: sees only backward, not forward.
