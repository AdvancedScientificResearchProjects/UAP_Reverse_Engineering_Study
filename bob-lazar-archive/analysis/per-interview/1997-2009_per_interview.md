## 1. 1997-09-26 — C2C with Art Bell (Comprehensive, ~3 hr)

This is the most detailed Lazar monologue of the era. Art Bell + Gene Huff co-present. Lazar is explicit and unhedged.

### 1.1 Biography / hiring

- **Los Alamos prehistory.** "Some years ago, I was working at Los Alamos National Labs in New Mexico." Meets Edward Teller outside a lecture hall. Teller is reading the local paper, which that day had a front-page article about Lazar's jet-engine car. [00:05:52–00:06:33]
- **"I had Q clearance, civilian top-secret clearance"** at Los Alamos [00:11:05].
- **Work at LANL:** "Initially, I was working on the polarized proton target on the accelerator… about half a mile" linear accelerator, beam split to experiments leased to scientists/countries. Also "some Star Wars development done there." [00:37:41–00:38:48]
- **Hiring path.** Mailed resume to Teller years later. Interview at EG&G by McCarran Airport; told overqualified; later called back for "a field propulsion system" position [00:09:26]. Initially imagined an ion drive / mercury-vapor electromagnetic propulsion.
- **Why him?** "22 active people working on it" [00:34:25]. Speculates that what mattered was his spare-time work (static ramjet, hydrogen car); interviewers asked about "play time." Psychological profile: "have you ever thrown tools?" [00:14:22].
- **Dates on project:** "December of 88 to April of 89." [02:19:17]
- **Supervisor:** Dennis Mariani — "stocky guy, smokes a cigar, very military-looking, short blonde hair," civilian clothes [00:30:09].
- **Co-worker:** Barry Castillo / Castile [01:09:17].
- **Previous worker fatality.** Read notes by the prior scientist; "there was a fatality, or actually three fatalities… they attempted to get into the reactor while it was in operation under load… the reactor did explode." [02:10:43–02:11:41]

### 1.2 S-4 facility

- ~10–15 miles south of Groom Lake [00:17:43]. Reached via "converted school bus, those big bluebird jobs… painted dark navy blue" from Groom Lake [00:17:54].
- Built into the side of a mountain, off Papoose dry lake bed; hangar doors around one side; main entrance on south face [00:28:44].
- Nine hangars / nine craft total [01:05:37].
- **Security signed:** 10-10 agreement (10 yr / $10k). Authorization order signed by Reagan [00:24:15]. Phone monitored — FBI showed up 30 min after a call with transcript of the call [00:26:53].
- Allergen skin tests and a wide-spectrum injection on first day [00:45:23]; Lazar collapsed with kidney pain shortly after (given Demerol) [00:49:04].

### 1.3 Sport Model craft — dimensions, hull, interior

- "about 52 feet in diameter, about 16 feet high" [01:56:35] (later with Testors refined to **52.8 ft** diameter).
- Nine craft total; only the "sport model" was one Lazar worked on.
- One damaged craft had a ~4-inch hole punched through a broad flat lip, metal bent outward — looked intentional, not a crash [01:06:28].
- Hull: cool to the touch, looked and felt like metal, could have been advanced ceramic [01:04:50]. American-flag sticker near the hatch [01:04:25].
- Interior: uniform "dull pewter-aluminum color"; "no sharp right angles anywhere"; "looks like it was just injection-molded"; "no seams" [01:53:47–01:54:44]. "Everything… fashioned out of wax, heated for a short time, and then cooled."
- Three levels.
  - Main level: central reactor, waveguide rising up to top of craft like a chimney, three seats facing one direction, three rectangular gravity amplifiers (consoles with no buttons/lights/switches/wiring) [01:56:41–01:57:35].
  - Lower level: three cylindrical **emitters** (two feet diameter, four feet long), hang down below, connected to the amplifiers above through short pipe mounts (~12 inches) that bend when an electric charge is applied but stay rigid, solid material, not telescoping [02:00:57–02:01:57].
  - Third area not accessed.
- Hexagonal collapsible floor hatch — "stuck your hand in the notch and pushed, and the entire thing collapsed flat and left a hole open" [01:59:11].
- One archway between seats has skin that goes transparent when energized — more metallic than crystalline [03:01:57–03:02:36]. Display scrolled text resembling Korean / Chinese writing [03:02:58].
- "No buttons, lights, switches, or wiring in the entire craft" [01:57:35].

### 1.4 Reactor

- "Extremely small, maybe two foot square, maybe six inches high" [01:40:25] (the plate); a half-sphere on top.
- Bottom plate "operated like a little cyclotron… the first cyclotrons were almost exactly that size." Particles accelerated in a circle, diverted by E and B fields up into the target (115) inside the dome [02:13:36–02:14:02].
- Output equivalent to a small nuclear power plant; no waste heat — "a violation of the laws of thermodynamics" [01:40:33].
- "Reactor always outputting its maximum… they change the phase of the amplifiers themselves" to 'throttle' [02:52:00].
- Gravity-field demo: when operating, hand pushed back like north-pole-to-north-pole magnets; golf ball ricocheted off the field [01:41:14–01:44:55]. Omnidirectional gravity field around the reactor sphere [01:45:22].

### 1.5 Element 115

- "A super heavy element, one that doesn't naturally occur on Earth and certainly one that we haven't synthesized" [02:15:21].
- "Stable element, one that was not undergoing any radioactive decay" [02:15:55].
- References theoretical **island of stability** around 114/115/116 [02:16:03].
- **Quantity:** "223 grams in the craft, in the reactor." [02:17:20]
- When asked if he took some out: "I really don't want to comment on that." [02:17:46]
- State-of-the-art reference (1997): "I believe the last isolated was 112… most of the more recent work has been done in Darmstadt, Germany." Plan to fuse curium + calcium for 116 [02:17:13–02:18:30].

### 1.6 Gravity propulsion physics

- **Amplifier ≠ emitter.** The emitter is the cylindrical device on a pivot — "quite similar to a horn on a microwave system" [01:35:36].
- "Gravity waves behave a lot more like microwaves… waveguides and things of that sort that you'd see in a microwave system" [01:36:00].
- Bending light demo: "focused into a small point and produced a little black dot in the air, hovering in the air"; "the only thing that can bend light is gravity" [01:45:58–01:46:34].
- Two flight modes:
  - **Omicron** — atmosphere/low performance. One amplifier below as an "anti-gravitational pillar to stand on"; one rotated 90° to create a gravitational distortion ahead of the craft. "Creates a distortion… a divot in time-space." "Craft would actually continuously slide downhill." [02:03:41–02:05:06]
  - **Delta** — interstellar travel. Belly-forward. "All three amplifiers are focused on a single point" creating intense gravitational distortion. "Instead of the craft actually flying, you're basically distorting the fabric of space around the craft and move without actually traveling." [02:06:19]
- "Gravity, space, and time are all interrelated. Around areas of intense gravitational field, time essentially slows down." [02:07:56]
- **Frequency / carrier.** "There was an apparent carrier wave… of the extremely low frequency… 7.46 Hertz at a one micron bandwidth" [02:50:22–02:51:04]. "Not just a simple electromagnetic wave."
- **Coherence / band.** "Narrow band phenomena"; gravity radiation is **coherent** [02:51:04].
- **Power.** "Gigawatts" [02:52:00].
- **Recycle / duty cycle (delta mode).** Multiple consecutive jumps — "you can't make a jump from here to 30 light years out" in one step; there is a recycle time (microseconds or a millisecond — couldn't recall) [02:53:03].
- **Torus field around craft** confirmed in exchange with Stan Dale: "in fact, in a torrid around the craft" [01:31:18]. Bottom extended as a pedestal; field surrounds the craft. "Directly under the craft… you will only see the sky above it" — light bent around, camouflage effect [02:55:00].
- Blue corona on lift-off from "high voltage present, at the very bottom… dissipated when the craft got up to about 30 feet" [03:01:00].
- **Candle-flame-in-distortion experiment.** Barry placed a candle in the localized gravity distortion — "the candle flame continued to emit light but the flame's flickering as if it was frozen in time." "This has really driven me crazy." [02:57:02–02:58:30]
- **Weapon / defensive potential.** "Use gravity as a lens… to focus energy weapons" — helps keep particle beams focused in atmosphere [02:17:55, reiterated in 2003].
- **Why gravitons aren't the answer.** "I don't believe there are gravitons. I don't believe there's particles involved." Physicists in the 80s thought gravitons; by 97 the orthodoxy had moved to gravitational waves, which Lazar claims vindicates him [00:47:56–00:48:34].

### 1.7 Demo of working craft

- "I was permitted to be inside the hangar. The craft was taken outside, and… the craft just lifted up the ground and moved around slightly. It didn't zoom around." [02:58:55–02:59:25]
- Ground-to-craft comms: operator used "a normal VHF transmitter around the 200 MHz bandwidth." [02:59:22]
- Humans would survive the exotic maneuvers because the gravity field isolates occupants [02:59:15].

### 1.8 Autopsy photo / "alien" glimpse

- Autopsy photo: Y-cut chest shot; a single large organ resembling a liver, "many other chambers… performed the functions of many organs." [02:43:43]
- Quick glance through wired security window: two techs in white coats and "a smaller thing" with its back to the door. "I find it much more plausible to believe that this was a dummy… trying to ascertain the size of whatever creature flew in these crafts." [02:45:28–02:47:01]

### 1.9 Records destruction

- Los Alamos and Kirk Mayer denied he had worked there; only remaining evidence: a 1982 Los Alamos newspaper front-page article and his name/phone in an old Los Alamos phone book [02:22:35–02:23:45].

## 2. 1998-01-15 — C2C with Art Bell + John Lear

Lazar is NOT a guest. Lear is solo; he retells Lazar's story, sometimes inaccurately. A different caller "Bob in Vegas" (not Lazar) phones in. Technical statements here are Lear's interpretations, not Lazar's. Key Lear-sourced statements (flagged as Lear, not Lazar):

- "Element 115 is the power source for these extraterrestrial craft. We've been able to synthesize up to, like, 106. But there is an element on up the line that is 115. And by pumping protons into 115, it becomes 116, which immediately decays. And what the result of the decay is, is antimatter. And when antimatter is mixed with matter, it's 100% conversion of matter to energy… and also out of this reaction, they pull off the gravity A wave." [01:26:39 — **Lear, not Lazar**]
- "Bob Lazar had his hands on some 115." [01:27:55 — Lear]
- "Gravity is instantaneous, and there's two forms of it, A and B… generate a very powerful beam of gravity and pull space towards you. Space is actually a fabric." [02:24:55 — Lear]
- "Security clearances: top secret, then 28 levels of crypto… above that 10 names like Umbra, Amber, Ultra, the top being Majestic. President has about top-secret-crypto-17." [02:30:10 — Lear]

This interview contributes no first-hand Lazar technical content for this era. It is useful only as a record of how Lear framed the "gravity A wave" and "115 → 116 → antimatter" summary that the UFO community subsequently attributed to Lazar.

## 3. 2003-12-06 — C2C with Art Bell ("UFOs & Alt Energy")

Lazar is back, solo, in New Mexico now. This interview predates the Feb 2, 2004 Dubna announcement of element 115 synthesis. There is therefore **no reaction to moscovium synthesis in this transcript** — the Russian work had not yet happened. Lazar still references only the Darmstadt (Germany) heavy-ion lab.

Note on the task brief: the premise that "Element 115 was just synthesized in Russia" during this 2003 interview is historically not correct — the Dubna synthesis was announced ~2 months after this show. The interview therefore contains Lazar's last public characterization of 115 before its formal lab synthesis.

### 3.1 Attitude / retrospective framing

- "I hate talking about UFOs… it's gotten pretty old… become really tough for people to take you seriously when you're known as the UFO guy." [00:00:10]
- Fond memories of pre-media Area-51 era: "We knew exactly what was going to happen… I had the test flight schedule at that time." [00:02:00]
- On other insiders coming forward: "just forget it… It's not worth the hassle. Nobody's gonna believe what you have to say anyway." [00:55:55]

### 3.2 Sport Model (re-description, consistent with 1997)

- Same injection-mold, one-color, no right-angles description. No seams. Gray. [00:07:04]
- Dimensions recalled as "52.8 feet in diameter or something like that" [00:33:12], confirming 1997 Testors number.
- No buttons/joysticks observed; navigation unknown. [00:08:00]

### 3.3 Propulsion

- "It's a gravity propulsion system. It's something that's completely alien to us." [00:09:50]
- Bowling-ball-in-bed analogy for gravity distortion [00:10:15].
- "Three gravity amplifiers… long tubes in the belly… on a universal pivot-type joint… swing two of the emitters up, focus on a point in front of the craft, cause a local distortion. Craft moves forward." [00:10:25]
- Modes: **Omicron** = one emitter for hover, two for forward distortion (atmosphere); **Delta** = all three focused on one point, belly-forward, for space [00:11:49–00:13:12].
- "UFOs at these ridiculous angles, at 45 degree angles, hanging in the middle of the air… the transition between the two different flight modes." [00:12:42] — new framing tying observed UFO photos to the omicron/delta transition.
- **Why no crashes intrinsically:** "I really can't see how one of these things could crash… if you're generating your own gravitational field, you're essentially immune to everything." Unless internal failure or piloting error [00:13:46–00:14:30].

### 3.4 Element 115 (2003 version)

- Atomic mass 115. Periodic table doesn't go that high (as of 2003). Caller notes [01:30:11].
- "Something unique about that element… something apparently happens different there… previous attempts to duplicate what was going on with other materials, other nuclear materials, and there was no success." [01:30:27]
- **Reactor detail (new framing):** "The way the reactor worked in the craft was like a small accelerator and it constantly bombarded the 115 which transmuted and immediately decayed and that's when it produced its gravitational pulses and as a by-product it produced a tremendous amount of heat." [01:32:25–01:32:42]
- "How the 115 itself was bombarded… releases a pulse of a gravitational wave, and kind of as a by-product releases a tremendous amount of heat, and that heat is converted to electricity." [01:33:27]
- State of the art: Darmstadt had been "sneaking up" on 115 using nucleus-on-nucleus collisions; "I don't think they reached it and in fact… somebody found out that there was false data. I mean the last heavy element that was claimed that might have been 113" [01:31:27–01:32:06] — likely referring to the Berkeley / Lund retracted 118 claims.
- Properties: "incredibly heavy and incredibly dense." [01:31:18]

### 3.5 Propagation of gravity wave

- Caller (physics student) asks propagation speed. Lazar: "the propagation is instantaneously." "Every way we attempted to measure the propagation from the reactor base to the emitters themselves, there was no delay at all." Possibly the wave's own gravitational field distorts the time-space around it and only appears instantaneous. [01:27:50–01:28:29]
- Envisioned communication application: instantaneous comms to Mars [01:29:00].

### 3.6 Microwave-like carrier in tuned pipes

- "Anytime inside the craft, the gravity, the basic gravity wave was routed anywhere from the reactor to the amplifiers, it always traveled inside tuned pipes… it almost seemed to propagate as microwaves did." [01:25:16]
- "If it's just a basic wave… you could just set up a high-frequency oscillator… microwaves, x-rays… you should just run into gravity at some point, but you don't, so there's something else there." [01:26:13]

### 3.7 Reactor-detonation incident

- Caller asks about an atomic explosion underground connected to a reactor. Lazar confirms: "I heard that officially too." Three fatalities. "They took it to the nuclear test site… done remotely underground… the people monitoring it apparently were too close and they didn't realize the explosion was going to be that large." Announced as a low-yield nuclear test; date was in March [01:45:52–01:48:10].

### 3.8 Weapons

- "A tremendous weapon potential here. Maybe more so than energy." [00:16:28]
- "Part of the briefing contained a couple hints about using gravity as a lens… to focus energy weapons… particle beams and other energy-type weapons disperse quickly in an atmosphere." [00:17:57]
- "Things that become possible are what we consider science fiction. Now the popular shields in Star Trek become possible." [00:19:22]
- "My own personal belief, absolutely" — we've built weapons from this tech [00:17:57].

### 3.9 Silo and polonium

- Owns a decommissioned nuclear missile silo outside Roswell with John Farratt. Silo's main missile-bore is ~200 ft deep. Cost of original government construction: "close to $13 million just for the concrete." Declines to say what he's doing with it [00:34:00–00:40:00].
- Hydrogen-fuel Corvette via metal hydride storage (patented), filled by solar-panel electrolysis over 2–3 days; ~700 mi range [00:47:22, 00:51:00]. Mentions lithium-6 deuteride as a thermonuclear-weapon-restricted hydride [00:52:58].

### 3.10 Craft was never flown outside Earth's atmosphere

- "Do you think these craft when tested were taken outside the Earth's atmosphere? No, you do not? No, I know that for a fact." [01:08:33]
- Reason: prized possession, limited fuel (115), risk of loss [01:09:53].

### 3.11 Other scientists' anti-gravity claims

- T. Townsend Brown, Searl, Schauberger, Otis Carr etc.: "It's not real. Please prove me wrong, come and check out what you're going to claim… no device of any sort that can create a gravitational wave now." [01:37:37–01:38:45]

### 3.12 Back-engineering status

- "As far as I knew back then, we failed." [01:08:30]
- Haven't seen obvious tech leaks into industry yet [01:34:59].

## 4. 2009-11-15 — C2C 20th Anniversary (George Knapp host, John Lear + Gene Huff guests)

**Lazar did not appear on the show.** Huff explains at [01:46:13]: "Bob really did was regretful… he was supposed to try and call in, but he was in transit over a 2,000-mile trip hauling three horses and three dogs and three cats and furniture in a four-vehicle caravan."

Therefore this interview contains **no new Lazar technical statements**. It documents what Lear and Huff remember and how they retold the story 20 years on. Important because the task brief asks specifically for retrospective new details; none are supplied by Lazar here. All technical claims in this transcript are second-hand from Lear/Huff.

### 4.1 Element 115 retrospective (Huff/Lear, not Lazar)

- Cloud-chamber experiment at Lazar's house: bell jar + dry ice + a sample of 115 in a lead "clamshell" holder; a Coleman-lantern mantle (thorium) as alpha source. Alpha particles were deflected. Videotaped (tape "somewhere… I just don't know where it is"). Participants: Lear, Joe, Ben, Eddie, Lazar, and Jim Taliani. [01:21:04–01:23:28]
- "Somebody gave it to him from Los Alamos. And we put out the story that he stole it. So we wouldn't implicate the guy that gave it to us from Los Alamos." Code-name memory: "LA 1000 or something" [01:21:14–01:21:27].
- 115 "wasn't just 115 like hydrogen is hydrogen. It was an isotope… had to be kept under neutron bombardment at rest" [01:27:41, 01:25:49].
- "Bob had the 115 in that lead casing, sort of with a bullseye on it in front of his particle accelerator" as a threat: if OFI came to kill him he would trigger an antimatter explosion [01:28:02–01:28:41].
- "Two were stolen… One we have no comment on." Bob Bigelow was "actually involved in" a recovery attempt. One stolen piece was recovered; the remaining piece was "buried at a place where… it would be hard to get, but it would be secure at that location for a long time. It would be obvious if its containment was ever jeopardized." [01:30:32–01:32:57]
- Scientists from Germany later sent Lazar a ~100-page physics formula about 115 [02:04:51].
- Lear's claim: naturally occurring 115 requires a much larger solar system; cannot be mined on Earth; too expensive to synthesize in quantity [01:29:37, 02:04:51].

### 4.2 Cold-chain of synthesized 115 (Huff)

- "Now there is [115]. However, the 115 that's been created in a lab is far different from what Bob Lazar described back then." [01:24:23] — This is the clearest retrospective acknowledgement in the corpus that post-2004 Dubna 115 (extremely short half-life) does not look like Lazar's stable 500-pound-scale 115. Huff says Bob's 115 was an isotope with different properties.
- Quantity implied: "500 pounds of this stuff" (Knapp phrasing). "500 pounds isn't a roomful. This is an element heavier than lead." [01:25:28]

### 4.3 Projects named for the first time (Huff at [02:01:19])

- **Project Galileo** — back-engineering of the disk (project Lazar was in).
- **Project Sidekick** — beam weapon, "with a gravity lens, if I remember right."
- **Project Looking Glass** — "a method of seeing back in time" (not forward).
- Huff adds: "seeing back in time for scientists, just being able to see back two or three seconds could make a big difference." [02:02:09]

### 4.4 Reactor / propulsion recap (Huff/Lear)

- "They'd actually bombard it with… a proton… sent down a tuned tube… to plug into the nucleus of the 115, which would bend up to 116. Yeah, make it 116. And then it would fission off some particles, some of which were antimatter. And this antimatter would be reacted with matter. And then the heat from that reaction would be converted to electricity." [01:26:06–01:26:37]
- "115 turns to 116, it decays, releases antimatter. The antimatter travels down a tuned tube… interacts with the matter that it interacts with to cause the antimatter matter explosion… That matter is gas." Huff claims an unnamed gas is the matter target [01:54:45–01:55:22].
- "Recycle that generator every 12 milliseconds" in delta mode (Lear) [01:48:53] — more specific than Lazar's 1997 "microseconds or a millisecond."
- Huff flags disagreement on record: "we know that earth scientists do not agree that gravity is a wave… we know that there should be so much waste heat from an antimatter reaction in a little reactor inside of a flying disk that everything would melt, and we understand that." [01:31:18]

### 4.5 Records destruction (Huff, recap)

- Kirk Mayer subcontractor, Z-number issued; Tom Hood did a timeline; Lazar needed a physics degree for LANL work of the kind he held [01:15:48].

### 4.6 Lear "new Area 51"

- Lear claims there is a new Area-51-class facility "about a third" bigger; "It's the new Area 51… it's 20 years old." Current craft may be there [01:01:39–01:03:13, 01:52:20]. This is Lear, not Lazar.

## Cross-interview summary (era 1997–2009)

- 1997 — richest direct-from-Lazar technical exposition of the era.
- 1998 — Lazar absent; Lear provides the commonly-quoted "gravity A wave / 115→116→antimatter" formulation.
- 2003 — Lazar confirms and extends 1997 material, adds new framing (UFO-at-45°-angle = omicron→delta transition, instantaneous gravity propagation measured inside reactor, reactor detonation at NTS, craft never left atmosphere).
- 2009 — Lazar absent; Huff/Lear name Galileo / Sidekick / Looking Glass publicly, and Huff explicitly notes the mismatch between Dubna 115 and Lazar's 115.
