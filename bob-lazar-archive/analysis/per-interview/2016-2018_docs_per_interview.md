# Lazar 2016 & 2018 Corbell Documentaries — Per Interview

Two Corbell-produced film transcripts: the 2016 short "Lazar: Cosmic Whistleblower" (File 38, 18 min) and the 2018 Netflix/Tubi feature "Bob Lazar: Area 51 & Flying Saucers" (File 40, 1h37m). Both mix narrator/voiceover, KLAS archival footage, and sit-down Lazar interviews. This document extracts only Lazar's own on-camera statements (and, where relevant, direct quotes from George Knapp and Joy Lazar that supply technical anchor-points the narrative otherwise lacks). Narrator voiceover, Corbell's poetic interstitials, and stock footage are excluded.

---

## 1. Lazar: Cosmic Whistleblower (2016-02-19) [File 38]

### Context
Corbell's 16-min teaser/short doc, released three years before the feature. Largely assembled from 1989 KLAS "Dennis" footage plus a new short sit-down with Lazar and an on-camera Knapp interview. Bulk of the new Lazar material is a reflective second-chance recap; two technical blocks are usable. The polygraph audio track is presented in full as a voiceover.

### Briefings / First Day
- [00:11:03–11:44] *"I had sent resumes to several national labs around the country. I got a response from a couple of them. They said, we might have something in the near future you would be very interested in. You know, you say you work on little projects, I said, yeah, I have a particle accelerator in my master bedroom and things of that sort. Some time went by and they called me back in and they said there was a senior staff physicist that was leaving this organization and they basically interviewed me for that job."*
- [00:11:41–11:46] *"The first day was when I read most of the briefings. 121 of these things."* — confirms the 121 briefings figure.

### Reactor / Gravity Physics (short technical block)
- [00:11:47–12:17] *"Through some reaction it produces a gravity field that's not completely understood. One of the things that they were teaching me was the physics that connect all this together. To create fields and power such as these disks required to do what they do, to lift off the ground without a body of propulsion system, requires a tremendous amount of energy. This is the only way they can achieve it."*
- [00:12:22–12:43] Antimatter energy-density framing: *"It's the most potent energy source that there can be. Just as a rough guesstimate, a kilogram of antimatter is equal in energy to 47 megaton hydrogen bombs. It's an extremely potent energy source. It's a direct conversion of matter to energy."* — the "47 megaton hydrogen bombs per kg of antimatter" figure is a reframed version of his older "one kg = 47 megatons" energy-density soundbite; specific wording not found in earlier 1989–2009 transcripts.

### Polygraph voiceover (audio track played in-film)
- [00:06:09–07:58] Polygrapher audio questions Lazar and he answers under test:
  - *"Did you knowingly lie when you said that you had actually seen anti-gravity propulsion in operation?"* — *"No."*
  - *"Did you knowingly lie when you said you had seen an anti-matter reactor?"* — *"No."*
  - *"Did you knowingly lie when you said that you actually saw a 30-odd-foot gravity-powered flying disk?"* — *"No."* (asked three times)
- This is the first time the polygraph audio has been played in a Corbell production; earlier references cite only the written conclusion.
- Note the polygrapher's sizing: *"30-odd-foot gravity-powered flying disk"* — contradicts the 52-ft canonical figure. Polygrapher's language, not Lazar's; see Contradictions.

### Re-affirmation block
- [00:14:49–16:29] *"I know there are alien craft here from another planet. I now, I saw other ones, but I was inside one. I know it was not made on earth. I know it was made with materials that we cannot fabricate. We cannot duplicate. And we've never been able to. I know it uses a power source that's so advanced that we could only dream of something along those lines. And the energy density on it is phenomenal… the installation itself was buried in the side of a mountain."*
- [00:16:22–16:29] *"I also know that at some point they've examined or had bodies of alien creatures somewhere."* — Lazar's firmest on-camera statement about alien bodies since 1991. Framed as briefing-material knowledge, not direct sighting.

### Intimidation / House (Corbell + Knapp, anchor points)
- [00:08:10–08:51] (Knapp) Break-in / mind-games pattern: doors, drawers, blackboard writing erased/added. Uzi-in-unlocked-car episode at gym retold: *"the doors to the car are open, the windows are down, the glove box is open, and the Uzi is laying there on the front seat. That wasn't a burglary, that was somebody messing with him."*

## 2. Bob Lazar: Area 51 & Flying Saucers (2018-12-04) [File 40]

### Context
Corbell's Netflix/Tubi feature. Opens with 1989 KLAS "Dennis" archive then repeatedly cuts to new Lazar/Joy/Knapp/Santacruz/hypnotherapist interviews plus an FBI-raid verité sequence. This is Lazar's single most polished long-form on-camera account between 1991 and 2019; it backstops or elaborates most claims that appear fully developed on JRE #1315 six months later.

### 2.1 Hiring & First Day
- [00:16:53–17:53] *"I had sent resumes to several national labs. I got a response from a couple of them. I went in for an interview. They had a job in mind, and then they continued questioning me mainly on my interests outside of work. They seemed to be really concerned about that. About things like jet cars? Right. What do you do in your spare time? You say you work on little projects. I said, yeah, I have a particle accelerator in my master bedroom and things of that sort. Some time went by and they called me back in. They said there was a senior staff that was leaving this organization, and they basically interviewed me for that job."*
- [00:17:41–17:57] *"I was given a lot of briefings to read. I believe there were 121 different briefings. They sat me in a room while they were going and updating my clearance to a level that they call majestic."* — first on-camera Lazar-voiced use of "majestic" since 1991.
- [00:17:57–18:09] Reaction: *"I was completely shocked. I couldn't believe it, but I was fascinated. I was so excited. It's a science dream, really."*

### 2.2 Reactor — Bench Demonstration (key technical block)
- [00:18:12–18:45] *"Eventually, I would have shown them one close-up, one operating. They had one of the reactors out of the crafts, which was an antimatter reactor. I was given a demonstration on how it worked, things that it did, and the physics of it. The thing they were most interested in is duplicating the reactors without using this element 115, which is, of course, impossible. There have been projects before that just trying to use a normal nuclear generator fueled with plutonium. It was really a futile attempt."*
- [00:31:46–32:13] *"The first time Barry showed me the reactor in operation here it is on the bench and he said try and touch the sphere on top. And you couldn't. Your hand was pushed away. Just like two light poles of a magnet. It's the exact same feeling. But there was no metal involved. And that's shocking. That's really shocking because nothing does that."* — canonical bench-repulsion demonstration; matches JRE1315 [00:07:50–09:40] almost verbatim.
- [00:32:10–32:37] Implication cascade: *"Powerful force field. So just seeing something like that immediately starts that whole chain reaction in your mind going wow wait if you can do this. There can be force fields on tanks. There can be things that lift off the ground. We don't have jet and rocket engines anymore. That means there's weight. There's no use for cars. I mean, boom! The whole thing changes."*

### 2.3 Victorian-Reactor Analogy (new framing)
- [00:30:55–31:42] *"I've given the example of transporting a small modern day portable nuclear reactor back into Victorian times and giving it to the scientists there. Back in that time they didn't even know about radiation. So they'd see a machine making power… and marvel at it. Wow it's producing a lot of power. There's no smokestack. There's no coal. There's no fuel… And they start taking it apart. Well they'll all die as soon as they get close to the core."* — NEW analogy not in earlier transcripts; previously used the steam-engine-to-18th-century analogy.

### 2.4 Propulsion / Field-Propulsion (core technical block)
- [00:49:47–51:25] *"I know that technology doesn't exist at all… Science doesn't even know what gravity is, much less how to produce it or control it. And here is a device that's producing it and controlling it and using it as propulsion… Everything that we have, whether it's a propeller plane or a jet or a rocket, it throws something out the back, either high-speed exhaust or a large volume of air. It's an action-reaction force… This is the first time there's a craft that's… It's a reactionless craft. It's a field-propulsion craft. And what it does is it creates a distortion in space and time in front of it, where space actually bends."*
- [00:51:09–51:25] *"Put a bowling ball in the middle of your bed, and then a foot in front of it, take your fist and push down on the mattress. The bowling ball will roll towards it. And that's exactly how the craft works. It creates a distortion right in front of it, and the craft falls forward."* — canonical mattress/bowling-ball analogy voiced on camera here; also used on JRE1315.

### 2.5 Sport Model Layout — full walk-through [01:06:00–01:10:09]
Long, detailed layout description delivered as he sketches. Quoted in full because it is Lazar's cleanest on-camera architectural account.
- *"This is the thing I termed the sport model. Underneath this floor, there are three large cylindrical devices. These are on mounts that allow them to completely swivel up to 180 degrees and in 360 degree rotation. Directly above each one is a small rectangular object. This is on the floor above. And these are the gravity amplifiers themselves."*
- *"In the very center, there is a small reactor. Surrounding this in three equally spaced areas are the amplifiers… Under these amplifiers, underneath and the floor below, are the gravity emitters. It's the reactor here powering the gravity amplifiers. Gravity amplifiers output goes into the gravity emitters at the bottom. The resulting gravity beam or anti-gravity wave can be pretty much put anywhere you want it to."*
- *"There was another level up here… I assume these to be some sort of navigational engine. People call these large black rectangular areas on the top portholes. I believe they were some planar sensor array that just took in information from the surrounding area, whether it be patterns of stars or what have you. There was their version of a computer… I don't know for a fact."*
- *"The center antenna is really an extension of the reactor in the center. That's a wave guide which allows the emission of the gravity wave, which forms kind of a heart shape over the whole craft. That's how it creates its distortion."*
- *"These gravity emitters can be swung all the way up to 180 degrees. This allows the craft to essentially stand on two of them and hover, while this one swings up and creates a distortion in front of it, allowing the craft to slide forward. That's how their low power mode Omicron configuration operate. The Delta configuration uses all three."* — FIRST on-camera naming of Omicron/Delta in the Corbell feature (names absent from most 2018–2019 material).
- *"Unlike science fiction movies where you see flying saucers just flying along like that, they actually fly belly first. The craft flies along, leaves the atmosphere of the planet, it turns its belly to the destination. The three amplifiers focus in on the destination and that's how it proceeds."*

### 2.6 Small Seats / Alien Scale
- [00:54:48–54:58] *"The most shocking thing was when I actually got to look inside the disk, the seats and the panel associated with them were very small. They were perhaps that far off the ground, like something small. Now, if we had made something like that, we wouldn't have made it to fit children."*
- [00:54:56–55:08] *"It just led me to believe, with the size of the alien cadavers I saw, the size of the seats inside."* — first verbal pairing of "alien cadavers I saw" with the small-seat observation on camera in this film; wording is slightly stronger than 1989–2009 corpus (he usually says "autopsy photos" or "briefing photos," not "saw").

### 2.7 Hand-Bone Scanner Vindication [00:34:50–37:26]
- Lazar's 30-year-old claim about a hand-bone biometric scanner at S-4 entry is visibly vindicated on camera when Corbell produces a photo from a declassified Nellis/Tonopah stealth-era article.
- *"The interesting thing is when you walk into the facility, or even to leave, they have a hand reader… a small plate with some pins on it that you could put between your fingers. There's a bright light above it… I was told that it has to do something where it measures… the bright light measures the bones in your finger. They're unique to each person."*
- *"I can't believe you found a picture of this. I really can't. This was the scanner used to get in to S4, and I tried to explain this to people so many times. There was pins in, you stuck your hand, a light above, and it supposedly measured the length of the bones in your hand that, you know, is unique in each individual. It's exactly, exactly how it was."*

### 2.8 Employer / Route to S-4 (narrated re-cap)
- [01:04:04–01:04:23] *"According to Lazar, his employer was the United States Navy. He says he and other government employees would gather near EG&G, fly to Groom Lake, and then a very few people would get into a bus with blacked out or no windows and drive to S4."* — matches LK19/JRE1315.
- [01:04:23–01:04:42] Lazar describing arrival at S-4: *"Very interesting building. It's got a slope of probably about 30 degrees, which are hangar doors. And it has textured paint on it, but it looks like sand. It's made to look like the side of the mountain that it's in, whether it's to disguise it from satellite photographs or what."* — note the 30° slope here vs ~60° in LT-91 (see Contradictions).

### 2.9 Drive Summary (narrator quoting Lazar)
- [01:01:47–01:02:13] *"The power source is an antimatter reactor. They run gravity amplifiers. There's actually two parts to the drive mechanism. It's a bizarre technology. There's no physical hookup between any of the systems in there. They use gravity as a wave, using wave guides, almost like microwaves."*

### 2.10 Polygraph Details (Terry Tavernetti)
- [01:02:13–01:03:05] NEW corroboration detail: *"Polygrapher Terry Tavernetti runs a corporate security operation and is a former Los Angeles police officer. He put Lazard through four tests and concluded there was no attempt to deceive."* Tavernetti on camera: *"When I left there thinking that I feel we do have some credibility to what the subject had to say."*
- *"Tavernetti sent the test results to a third polygrapher who agreed the results appeared truthful."* — the "third independent polygrapher" detail is new in this film.

### 2.11 Hypnosis — full sequence [00:55:12–01:01:10]
- Lazar confirms the hypnotherapy happened with Layne Keck: *"Yeah, that's correct. With Lane Keck."*
- Purpose: *"I basically wanted to try everything I could to prove what I was saying. So this is just another tool… I wanted to recall as much as I possibly could. I mean, so much went through my mind. Just imagine all the things you were seeing. And every one is completely fascinating and something you haven't seen before. It's just data overload. So I was hoping that hypnosis would get me relaxed enough where maybe everything was packed so tight together. Maybe I could just start thumbing through things and just pick up even a couple little more tidbits."*
- Lazar: *"Yeah, technical things and probably others too."*
- Keck, on camera with Corbell, relates a specific new detail: under hypnosis Lazar drew a reactor diagram but drew it sideways — his subconscious was rendering what he had seen in the briefing book literally, including orientation. After coming out of hypnosis: *"he looked at it, he came back over and sat down here for a while and, oh, that's because all he had to do was turn it."* Framed as evidence of literal, image-faithful memory retrieval.
- The full induction script ("take a nice deep breath… double your relaxation and the number 100 will be there… one, zero, zero…") is played on film but adds no technical content.
- [00:55:56–55:59] NEW claim: *"I was told the rhythmic shouting and the constant threats, that's a form of hypnosis."* — Lazar implies S-4 handlers were actively inducing a hypnotic state via their yelling/threatening mode during briefings. First explicit framing of the abusive atmosphere as deliberate hypnotic technique.

### 2.12 Hangar Sighting / "The Kids"
- [00:46:47–48:28] *"I walked down the hallway. At one time I was working out there and the doors that go to the hangars, the smaller doors from the corridors, have a 9-inch or 12-inch square window with little wires running through it, just about head level. As I was walking by, I just glanced in… there were two guys in white lab coats facing me towards the door. They were looking down and talking to something small with long arms."*
- *"I don't think I saw an alien at S4… I'm still convinced I looked in the window and I think these guys had a doll in a small chair… I think they were just looking at dimensions and they put something in there… Again, we're talking about, you know, like a 400 millisecond glance."*
- [00:48:35] NEW detail (Corbell asks, Lazar confirms): *"But they did have a nickname for the aliens. The Kids."* — the nickname "The Kids" is first heard on camera in this film; had not appeared in the 1989–2009 corpus.

### 2.13 Element 115 — Full Machining Block [01:20:30–01:28:32]
- [01:25:25–25:31] *"Element 115 was what we would call the fuel, what provided the power for the reactor to work."*
- [01:25:32–25:42] *"Element 115 affects gravity. Element 115 produces its own gravitational energy."*
- [01:25:43–25:58] *"It had a very specific manufacturing technique. I don't really know how that information came to be. Its code name was LA-1000. That's what it was referred to offsite."* — "LA-1000" is a NEW code name first disclosed in this film. Also: *"was supposed to be an advanced armor. So it's an unusual material. So now we can take it to a national lab. This is LA-1000 classified material. It's a very advanced armor that takes care of all weird questions."*
- [01:26:14–27:45] Manufacturing: *"This fuel is in the form of a just a three-dimensional thin triangle, little rounded edges. It's somewhat copper in color, you know, that reddish-brownish. The way this is manufactured is really critical. It's not just cut out of a heavy sheet of material. It's, you know, a cylinder of this material is taken. This is machined into a cone… the outer part is shaved off using a lathe or something like that until you have a three-dimensional cone. Once you have the cone, it's sliced like this. And these slices become that."*
- [01:27:33–27:50] *"Now, even this starting piece, a solid cylinder, is not even a solid cylinder. It consists of many disks stacked up. And these are fused together to produce that. And all of these steps are necessary to produce a successful triangle that brings fuel… Now, apparently, if you don't do all this, that doesn't work."* — The "stacked disks → fused cylinder → machined to cone → sliced at angles into triangles" sequence is given here in the cleanest form; matches the Rachel-93 account but is more detailed and filmed on camera.
- [01:28:08–28:32] *"With the alien technology that was present in the craft, it takes that basis, that extra gravitational energy in a small reactor, amplifies it through the equipment there, directs it through the waveguides and archways into the emitters, and allows it to propel the craft and manipulate the gravity wave for whatever use they want."*

### 2.14 Element 115 Origin / Cloud-Chamber Tape (Knapp disclosure)
- [01:14:19–18:15] Corbell asks Lazar directly on record: *"Did you ever get a piece of element 115 out of Los Alamos?"* Lazar refuses to answer, turns off phones, leaves the record.
- [01:14:19–21:03] (Knapp narration / on-camera) Knapp discloses he found the lost cloud-chamber video tape in Lazar's personal home archive: *"I went through every tape that Bob had in his personal home archive. And one of those tapes on the outside in tiny, tiny little scribble said cloud chamber and low and behold, there's about just less than a minute of that cloud chamber test on tape."*
- Knapp also describes consulting international heavy-element physicists on stability: *"I called some of the top heavy element physicists on the planet from Russia to up in San Francisco to Washington, DC… three in particular who would really talk to me in depth about it. All of them across the board said, you cannot rule out a stabilized version of element 115. In fact, we believe it is theorized that it in the island of stability, that it would and could, and probably does exist."*

### 2.15 FBI Raid — Michigan (2017) [01:10:17–01:20:00]
- Lazar narrates the 2017 FBI raid on his Michigan facility (United Nuclear) on camera, with video footage of the scene.
- [01:16:00–17:40] *"The conveyor belt of vehicles and agents and police did not stop. The whole thing was like a twilight zone episode… there were a lot. A lot. Standing room only and in the building."*
- *"They had a forensic truck. They had a bunch of different agents. They gridded off the building."* Agencies listed: FBI, state police, FDA, NRC, bomb squad, computer squad, biohazard / hazmat.
- *"They said, they were looking for some paperwork, an old order from two years ago, but a customer that, you know, ordered some potentially toxic material, which they could have called for, but, this was certainly way over the top."*
- [01:17:40–17:50] Second raid: *"So you've been raided two times? Twice."*
- [01:20:05–21:20] Corbell asks point-blank whether the raid was about the Los Alamos 115 sample. Lazar: *"No, do not feel comfortable addressing that. No, I don't feel comfortable."* — notable; he actively declines rather than denies.
- [01:18:24–18:34] Beryllium on the bench visible in the film: *"So this is beryllium? Yeah. And what is it used for again? It's used in aerospace. It's very lightweight, quite strong and high temperature metal."*
- [01:20:11–21:04] Knapp on raid motive: *"that raid that happened in Michigan… They weren't there looking for records about some customer he had a couple of years ago. They were looking for 115."*

### 2.16 Reverse-Engineering Mandate / Futility
- [01:05:37–05:51] *"We were to reverse engineer the power and propulsion system of this craft and see if it can be duplicated with available materials."* — first clean on-camera statement of the mandate in this era.
- [00:18:30–18:45] Mandate described as impossible: duplicating reactors *"without using this element 115, which is, of course, impossible."*

### 2.17 Prior Raid (New Mexico)
- [01:11:02–12:30] Mario Santacruz segment references an earlier raid in New Mexico — *"the fire department, police, the zoning commission, everybody descended on our house."* Over a hydrogen-car project; separate from the 2017 FBI raid. Article headline: "Lab Supplier in Controversy."

He tells them the same story, where he went to school, where he worked, S4, MIT, Caltech, all that. And they investigated. They were having the same problems that I was having. They couldn't verify a lot of this stuff."*

And as a result of that, they were going to recommend… that he do hard time, go to prison. He knew it. That was the time for him to come clean… He stuck to his story."* — this story has been referenced elsewhere but not filmed on-camera with this level of detail.
