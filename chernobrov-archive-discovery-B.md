## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (300 words)

Vadim Alexandrovich Chernobrov (17 January 1965 – 18 May 2017) was a Russian aerospace engineer (MAI candidate of technical sciences), founder-coordinator of Kosmopoisk (Космопоиск, "Spacesearch"), and the principal Russian researcher into what he called "temporal field physics." His research program ran from 1988 until his death from blood cancer (leukemia) at age 51.

**Core technical claims:** Starting 7–8 May 1988, Chernobrov and Kosmopoisk built a series of seven "Lovondatr" (Ловондатр) devices — nested concentric electromagnetic spheres (called "electromagnetic working surfaces," ERP/ЭРП). The first model was ~90 cm external diameter with an 11.5 cm inner payload chamber. The seventh (2001) was 2.1 m external / 1 m payload. All operated on the principle of converging electromagnetic waves creating a quasi-monopole at the center, hypothetically reducing local vacuum energy density and slowing time. Measured results: 0.5 – 1.5 seconds per hour deceleration (best lab case: 4 minutes per 8 hours, or −30 s/hr). August 2001 human trial at Volgograd forest: 3% time reduction detected with quartz oscillators vs. reference clocks. Device also showed measurable thrust (10 g for 400 g model). Personnel in proximity reported physiological symptoms resembling "Philadelphia Experiment" accounts.

**Crop circle research:** Kosmopoisk investigated 900+ pictogram cases, confirming 158 as non-manmade. Authenticated circles show: stalks bent without breaking, continuing to grow horizontally; rapid formation (~10 seconds); cell vacuoles growing asymmetrically (faster on one side of stalk); thermal differences vs. surrounding field (thermal imaging used). First use of field microscopy on crop circles credited to Chernobrov. 

**Published output:** 34–50+ books (sources vary), key journal articles in *Tekhnika Molodezhi* (Feb 2002) and *Novaya Energetika* #3 (2003), patent application #2003110067 (RU). No confirmed *Populyarnaya Mekhanika* articles found — the specific magazine the user cited could not be verified despite multiple search approaches.

**Death / UAP link:** Official cause: leukemia. Speculative theories link illness to radiation exposure at anomalous zones (confirmed 2008 Kola Peninsula expedition entered radioactive zone). French authors Garijo (book: *KGB UFO File*) specifically attribute his cancer to visiting alleged UAP landing sites.

## SECTION 3: TECHNICAL DEVICE DESCRIPTIONS

### Lovondatr Device Series (Ловондатр) — Complete Technical Profile

**Operational principle:** Convergent electromagnetic waves launched from nested spherical surfaces arrive simultaneously at center, creating a quasi-monopole condition. Theory: reduces vacuum energy density → slows local time. Chernobrov's electromagnetic theory of time treats time as a manifestation of electromagnetic forces under specific geometric field conditions.

**Device generations:**

| Model | External Ø | Internal Ø | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| 1 (1988) | ~90 cm | 11.5 cm | First prototype; "basketball-sized" inner chamber |
| 2–6 | — | — | Intermediate versions; mouse experiments |
| 7 (2001) | 2.1 m | 1 m (payload) | Human-capable; nested: 30cm / 1m / 2.1m spheres |

**Measurement methodology:**
- Electronic clocks (multiple redundant)
- Quartz oscillators (separated, thermally isolated pairs)
- Mechanical clocks
- Atomic clock comparison
- Fiber-optic diode arrays (light propagation speed measurement)
- Radio reference time signal comparison

**Claimed results (experimental):**

| Metric | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Typical deceleration | 0.5–1.5 s/hr | Laboratory conditions |
| Best lab deceleration | 4 min / 8 hrs (= 30 s/hr) | Single exceptional case |
| Acceleration (observed) | up to +0.5 s/hr | Less stable, shows sharp jumps |
| Normalized range | +0.99 < t/t₀ < +1.01 | Published in journal |
| Human trial (Aug 2001) | 3% time reduction | Forest site, Volgograd; car batteries |
| Mouse differential | 40 s / 3600 s session | Mice aged 40 s less than controls |
| External field falloff | 1/r³ | Time change at distance |
| Thrust (side effect) | 10 g for 400 g model | Measured propulsive effect |

**Human trial (August 26, 2001):**
- Location: remote forest, Volgograd Oblast
- Power: car batteries (low capacity)
- Duration: 108 minutes (dog "Lunokhod"); human participants ~5–30 min
- Participants: 8 humans; 7 of 8 reported sensory effects
- Reported effects: visual phenomena, pulse acceleration, tingling, sensation of simultaneous past/present perception
- Personnel at proximity: reported "painful symptoms analogous to Philadelphia Experiment descriptions"

**Earliest experiment date:** Night of 7–8 May 1988 (stated as official start of experiments)

### Crop Circle Physical Measurements (Chernobrov/Kosmopoisk)

**Research scope:** 900+ cases investigated; 158 confirmed as non-manmade (authentic rate ~3:100 per Chernobrov)

**Physical characteristics of authenticated circles:**
- Formation time: approximately 10 seconds (based on witness accounts and analysis)
- Stalks: bent without fracture; continue horizontal growth for extended period
- Cell biology: vacuoles on one side of stalk grow faster than the other (asymmetric growth → bending without breakage)
- Thermal: temperature differences vs. surrounding field detected by thermal scanner
- Geographic pattern: 5 bands converge toward Stonehenge (UK); northern band meets at Komi Republic (~15 standing stones)
- Authentic vs. fake distinction: Kosmopoisk used field microscopy (first researchers to do so) and thermal scanning to differentiate

**Note on molecular structure:** User query specifically asked about molecular structure analysis. Available sources confirm cell-level observations (vacuole asymmetry) and growth behavior, but peer-reviewed molecular-level data (e.g., crystalline structure, polymer chain changes in cellulose) was not found in accessible materials. The 2011 book *Krugi na Polyakh v Rossii i SSSR* (PDF: kosmopoisk.org/images/stories/_files-users/lion/book_cropcircles/knp.pdf, 72pp, 8.7MB) is an image-only PDF and requires OCR to extract.
