## 1. B-PV-UG — «Подземные взрывы — кто дырявит планету» (`536016_Podzemnye_vzryvy.txt`, 31 KB)

**Full title:** Подземные взрывы — кто дырявит планету.
**Format:** FB2 (LibRusEc kit, FictionBook Editor 2.6, 2007-06-11). UUID `74FB94B3-0F2D-44F1-BD2E-8B9A8197E98A`, v1.1.
**Main thesis:** Chronicle of anomalous ground-explosion craters (March 1999 Ushakovo / Fatezh / Lobazovka events in Kursk Oblast). Author documents 8 fresh craters, dismisses meteorite/unexploded-ordnance/landslide/micro-earthquake/secret-weapon/UFO explanations in sequence. **Ловондатр / MV cross-refs:** None. No mention of Time Machine, ЭРП, Lovondatr, or MV hardware.
**Medveditskaya / Kosmopoisk / MAI cross-refs:** Kosmopoisk explicitly present — "спустя месяц… из Москвы приехали и мы, несколько специалистов «Космопоиска» с целым чемоданчиком физических приборов". Field team conducted independent measurements vs. MinChS. No MG or MAI reference.

**Numerical claims (selected):**
- Ushakovo crater: 13×8 m, depth 5 m, ~4000 t ejected earth; ~70–80 m from witnesses; ~1 km from pond witnesses.
- 8 craters in Fatezh & Oktyabrsky districts, tens of km apart (not 2–3 km as officially stated).
- Radiation in crater: 2× background, "not dangerous".
- Apr 1961 Korb-ozero crater: ~1500 m³ missing frozen earth; green-tinted ice shards, hollow black spherules.
- May 1967 Marliac, France: pits with purple fused quartz + alumina, formed above 1500 °C; clover leaves unscorched.
- 1968 Uppramen lake, Sweden: triangular holes in ice, scaly substance on bottom.
- 12 Apr 1991 Sasovo: ~30 m diameter, 4 m deep crater; windows shattered "в радиусе нескольких километров".
- Jun 1992 Sasovo: second crater 12 m × 4 m (silent, no flash).
- May 1994 Kursk NPP region: 2 craters ~7–8 m × 3 m; equivalent ~500 kg TNT; no explosive residue; "огненное тело" + columns of fire observed prior.

**Dated events:** 3–4 March 1999; April 1961; May 1967; 1968; 12 April 1991; June 1992; May 1994. "Труд" 18.03.1999 p.1.
**Named persons:** Василий Жиров (witness); Г.Бахирев, Е.Таллер (VNIIZiZPE soil scientists, МinChS commission).
**Notes:** This is a Kosmopoisk-field-investigation report; positions Kosmopoisk as the independent scientific party versus military/MinChS, and ties author personally to the crater ("я бесстрашно находился внутри воронки"). The "без аналогов за весь период многовековых наблюдений" February 1999 thunderstorm is flagged as a possible trigger — a tentative atmospheric-electrodynamics linkage left unresolved.
