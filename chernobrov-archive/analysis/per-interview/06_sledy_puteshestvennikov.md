**EN:** "Most probably they are visitors from the future, but they NEVER say so — absolutely never. I have never met a case in ufological practice where visitors called themselves that."

| Era | Cover identity |
|-----|----------------|
| Pre-20th c. | Angels / God's messengers / Virgin Mary |
| Early–mid 20th c. | Martians |

## 8. The fraud / non-logical-tail signature

### [01:40:49]–[01:44:34] — The long-distance-truck-driver case

Specific case (verdict: cover-story demonstration):
- Location: **highway M8 Arkhangelsk–Vologda–Moscow**, snow-covered stretch, wintertime

## 9. Disinformation strategy

**RU:** «прежде всего цель эта состоит в том, чтобы максимально продлить для себя период, для своей стороны, период монопольного изучения этой проблемы. То есть, чтобы потенциальный соперник, потенциальный противник не изучал, чтобы он относился к этому максимально серьезно, нужно всячески демонстрировать эту несерьезность, всячески ее накачивать, всячески обстебывать, опускать значимость, подбрасывать массу фейков и вообще… превращать всю серьезную проблему в балаган.»

## 10. Separation of "alien" vs. "inter-temporal"

### [01:14:13]–[01:17:27] — Why most "aliens" are actually time-travellers

Criterion-chain:
1. Human-shaped
2. Converse face-to-face without protective glass → same microbial environment → same biology
3. Writing uses known human scripts (Russian, Arabic, English) not invented alien runes [01:16:42]
4. Therefore almost certainly human, almost certainly from the future — assuming technology exceeds current secret programs ([01:16:16]: "as an engineer who has built classified devices, I can say these are not ours").

### [01:57:26]–[01:57:36] — Key distinction

**RU:** «между инопланетными и иновременными, как вы догадываетесь, есть огромная разница. Если иновременным категорически нельзя влиять на историю, то инопланетным, что называется, тут есть варианты.»

**EN:** "Between extraterrestrials and extra-temporals there's a huge difference. Extra-temporals absolutely must not alter history; extraterrestrials on the other hand — there are options."

## 12. "Работа на опережение" — search by proactive modelling

### [02:18:38]–[02:20:06] — Kosmopoisk's unnamed program

Focus: **aварийные пункты эвакуации** ("emergency evacuation points"). Logic: like any transport, a time-travel programme must have last-resort extraction procedures. Chernobrov claims Kosmopoisk has run this programme for several years.

### [02:27:58]–[02:51:57] — Criteria for where to locate an evacuation point

Requirements:
1. Reachable across very long geological / historical windows (not on sinking/rising islands, not on frontlines, not across shifting borders)
2. Ideal candidates named: **Ural or Altai mountains** [02:46:23]
3. Not in carlicom (microstate) Europe — border churn makes access unstable
4. **Russia's large territory is the optimum** for Eurasian extractions [02:46:39]–[02:50:19]
5. One point should serve **thousands of years** — [02:44:49]: «Некоторые эвакуационные пункты, возможно, существуют на протяжении сотен тысяч лет»

### [02:36:07]–[02:43:10] — "The name-name village" anecdote

A single known settlement located **strictly midway between St Petersburg and Moscow**, surrounded by bogs, accessible by one road. He declines to name it publicly to avoid drawing visitors. The place-name is "говорящее для путешественников во времени" — reads obvious to anyone who knows chrono-nav lore, unremarkable to locals. In the village allegedly lives:

> «максимум живёт две старухи и один странный старик лет так под 300 возрастом, который на поясе носит то ли какой-то древний пистолет, то ли бластер из будущего» [02:42:46]

Translation: "two old women and one strange 300-year-old man wearing what is either an ancient pistol or a future blaster on his belt."

This is presented as an actual target of Kosmopoisk investigation, not a joke.

### Checkable reference [02:51:17]

Points to his book *«Энциклопедия загадочных мест России»* ("Encyclopedia of Mysterious Places of Russia") as catalogue of candidate evacuation points.

## 13. Gaiduchok case — integrated detail

### [02:31:22]–[02:34:14]

- Moved to **Leningrad**, there **met H.G. Wells** in person; content of conversation unknown
- (Reinforces: the survival-in-woods beacon strategy is unreliable; hence need for evacuation point)
