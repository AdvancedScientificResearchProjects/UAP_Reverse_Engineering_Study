# PART 2 — NLO-DPL-24 · НЛО — доля правды, домысла и лжи (2h24, Dubna lecture)

The ASR has ~28 min of recycled subtitle-edit placeholder at the head — the actual talk starts at **[00:28:52]**. Audience is in Dubna ("город физиков-ядерщиков" / "city of nuclear physicists" [01:22:41]). Structure: a taxonomy of what is **not** a real UFO, then what is. This is Chernobrov's **explicit fraud-detection methodology**.

## 2.1 The 99%-vs-1% frame

### Opening taxonomy [01:00:04]–[01:00:15]

Chernobrov states: «**Большая часть**, подчеркиваю, большая часть всех изображений, которые мы получаем, они банальные.» / "The majority of images we receive are banal." He explicitly commits to **99% explainable / 1% residual** throughout (multiple restatements at [01:09:13], [01:27:55], [01:32:46], [01:33:01]).

### Exhibits A–G: taxonomy of "not a UFO"

Seven causative categories Chernobrov hand-identifies from sample photos the audience brings, in decreasing order of frequency / increasing rarity:

#### A. Lens-flare on Red Square — the three-factor forensic [00:28:52]–[00:33:29]

Case study: a famous "UFO over St. Basil's" clip. Chernobrov reconstructs it as requiring **three simultaneous conditions** (remove any one → effect disappears):

1. **Cheap camera + motion shooting** — camera in a moving car.
2. **Light mist** at St. Basil's end of Red Square — explained by **aerodynamic-tube** geometry of Red Square (Kremlin wall + GUM wall → N→S wind channel → airflow narrows around St. Basil's → compression → mist).
3. **Ice rink with spotlights** (built ~2008–2009 onward, seasonal). Rink acts as «огромное зеркало сто метровой величины, лежащее в самом центре нашей страны» / a ~100 m mirror at the very centre of the country. Spotlights bounce off the ice at grazing angles, illuminating the mist.

**Methodological point [00:33:06]–[00:33:29]:** identification is forensic-combinatorial — one must explicitly enumerate all **three** contributing conditions to reproduce the artefact. "Remove any one and the image would look completely different."

#### B. Rocket-launch effects [00:33:46]–[00:41:50]

Not all launch effects look alike; a specialist can identify **rocket type, origin base, mission class (ballistic / cosmic / manned), normal vs abort launch** from shape. Conditions: dark observer, lit upper atmosphere at rocket altitude (few hundred km → out of Earth's shadow). Visible **thousands of km** away. Chernobrov says he routinely posts on internet ahead of planned night launches in summer — a wave of "UFO" reports follows, confirming the prediction.

**Methodological tool:** check the public launch schedule **before** classifying the report.

#### C. Lenticular / disc-shaped clouds [00:41:50]–[00:43:20]

Require terrain: hills, mountains. Moscow region mostly deprived of them (a rare case was observed during the **Chelyabinsk-meteorite search expedition** in the Urals). Typical for Crimea, Black Sea coast.

#### D. "Shnek" / "skyfish" — motion-blurred insects [00:44:16]–[00:48:15]

Elongated / veretenoid / spiral objects; in Japan named **«скайфиши» (skyfishes)**. Explanation: **motion blur of flying insects** (butterfly, moth, fly) during non-instantaneous shutter exposure — several wingbeats captured as one. Scale illusion: seen against sky → "km-long ship"; against leaves → understood as tiny. Chernobrov's point: the scale judgment is forced by the background.

#### E. Shaped orbs / "obry" / "shongs" / "plasmoids" / "souls of the dead" [00:48:29]–[00:50:45]

Chernobrov gives an **explicit reproduction recipe**: flash on + fog/dust/water droplets in **defocus distance** (~tens of cm in front of lens) → guaranteed orb. Two test methods: (1) spray water from a plant spritzer, (2) shake dust from a rag while photographing with flash.

#### F. Halos / rainbow-family atmospheric phenomena [00:51:34]–[00:57:04]

Includes the **"Million for a UFO" case**: TV-channel jury, where Chernobrov was the identification member. A woman filmed a light object over Kamchatka, quite competently — steady camera, correct horizon framing. She was about to win the 1,000,000 ₽ prize. At the last moment Chernobrov ruled it a **halo variant** (rare subtype). Prize not awarded.

**His camera-hygiene rules (for future genuine captures) [00:54:35]–[00:55:24]:**
1. **Don't shake** — rest hand on something solid, ideally use a tripod.
2. **Do not stop recording** after the object leaves frame — pull the zoom back to **include horizon**, for scale referencing.

#### G. Chinese lanterns, advertising search-lights, satellites, first aircraft [01:00:21]–[01:02:10]

Historically each novel sky technology (Chinese lanterns at weddings, commercial searchlights ~15 years before lecture, first satellites, first aeroplanes) generated a UFO-report wave. Each wave eventually transitions to the "explained" column.

#### H. ISS live-stream artefacts [01:02:10]–[01:08:05]

Newest category (few years before the lecture). Anyone can watch ISS cameras 24/7 on-line. Objects passing the ISS frame are mostly **dust grains sunlit** / **space debris**. Famous recent case: a **thermal cover detached from a Russian ISS segment** (tied with cords a decade earlier → cords degraded → cover flew free). Because **binocular vision works only up to ~4 m** [01:04:50]–[01:05:09], scale judgement at arbitrary distance is impossible **without background reference**. In space, with **no fog / trees / ground** behind, a sunlit speck near the lens can be confused with a giant ship.

### Threshold rule

**Chernobrov [01:08:14]:** "When past one MCB a week we would say 'maybe genuine', but when **hundreds pass in a day**, 99% are dust / debris." This is his **frequency-threshold heuristic** for rejection.

## 2.2 The residual 1% — what Chernobrov counts as a real UFO

### Definition [01:29:38]–[01:31:09]
